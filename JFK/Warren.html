Warren Commission

The Warren Commission was created a week after the assassination of President John F. Kenedy. It was formed by his successor, Lyndon B. Johnson. The purpose of the commission was soley on the investigation of the assassination. The commission was led by Chief Justice Earl Warren and lasted nearly a year long.

The Conclusion

Once the nearly year long investigating ended, they concluded that the only shooter in the JFK assassination was Lee Harvey Oswald. However, some found this report contraversal. (See Afterthoughts)

Afterthoughts

Altough the report was supposed to confrim to the public that this wasn't a conspiracy and allow the American public to rest and know that the 35th president was assassinated by one man who wanted to see the president dead. However, these conclusions didn't satisfy the public. Thus, conspiracy theorist still believed that the presidents assassination was not a one man job.
